# ADR 0019: Thread Intelligence automatic publication

**Date:** 2026-07-09
**Status:** Accepted product decision. The revised design was approved on
2026-07-09; the implementation and Task 12 live gate are complete pre-flip, but
default-on graduation remains gated by the rest of its evidence and rollback
criteria. Both production feature defaults are still `false`.
**Supersedes:** The human-only constraints in `PHASE_4_PLAN.md` Gate A,
conditional/deferred scope, and Milestone 8 only for the bounded Thread
Intelligence surface defined here. It does not rewrite historical Phase 4
acceptance or authorize generated posts, moderation actions, or private-content
processing.

## Context

Phase 4 intentionally shipped a human-controlled community-memory foundation:
manual sourced summaries, curated/deterministic related topics, wiki revisions,
and deterministic since-last-read context. Its plan prohibited auto-published
machine-written canonical content absent a separate privacy, quality, cost,
retention, and human-approval decision. ADR 0003 then carried automated context
forward behind a dark flag.

The product owner has now selected a follow-on Thread Intelligence design:
AI-generated Living Briefs and related-topic explanations for sufficiently
active public threads, generated by a debounced background worker and published
automatically only after local validation. The member-specific "Since you last
read" surface remains deterministic and local.

The detailed contract, schema, workflows, acceptance thresholds, and rollback
requirements are in
`docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-09-thread-intelligence-graduation-design.md`.

## Decision

1. Under the approved eventual default-on posture, eligible public threads may
   receive an AI-generated Living Brief when the operator configures a provider
   credential. Until that graduation, both owning flags remain default-off.
   Private/hidden boards,
   pending/deleted content, account metadata, DMs, reports, and moderation notes
   are never provider input.
2. A validated result may publish without per-generation human approval. Local
   schema/source/candidate checks, the canonical Markdown sanitizer, content
   controls, output moderation, a current visibility/snapshot recheck, and an
   atomic publication transaction are mandatory; failure at any gate publishes
   nothing.
3. Curators retain control: they may edit, retire, restore, refresh, pause, and
   resume. A curator-edited summary is supplied as the next generation's
   baseline. Curated related-topic rows are authoritative and cannot be
   overwritten by an AI overlay.
4. Failure, missing credentials, pause, or budget exhaustion preserves the last
   good publication and never exposes an error to members. With no published
   manual or AI summary, no empty Living Brief renders.
5. Members see AI/curator attribution, update time, version, and cited source
   posts. Model, prompt version, response ID, token/reasoning usage, retries, and
   safe failure codes remain in an administrator-only evidence ledger.
6. Provider processing is replaceable. Generation, output moderation, and HTTP
   exchange sit behind `ThreadIntelligenceProvider`,
   `ThreadIntelligenceOutputModerator`, and `OpenAiTransport`. The initial
   implementation uses the OpenAI Responses and Moderations APIs, an
   environment-only credential, `store: false`, fixed provider hosts, no tools,
   and no automatic more-expensive-model fallback.
7. Raw prompts, raw provider responses, and duplicate post bodies are not
   retained. The ledger keeps bounded metadata, source/candidate IDs, request
   fingerprints, usage, outcomes, and summary lineage required for audit and
   rollback. Published-generation metadata follows the source thread's
   lifetime; unpublished attempts expire after 90 days, except unresolved
   dead/review-required evidence is retained until resolution and then for 90
   days.
8. `community_memory` and `automated_context` remain default-off until the
   design's security, privacy, quality, browser/no-JS, accessibility, worker,
   migration, backup/restore, live-evaluation, and rollback gates pass. The
   later default-on commit requires its own recorded evidence.

## Implementation and Evidence Status

The pre-flip implementation is present in migration `0077`,
`src/Worker/ThreadIntelligenceWorker.php`, the
`src/Service/ThreadIntelligence/` provider/validation/operations seams,
`templates/partials/living_brief.php`, and
`templates/admin/thread_intelligence.php`. The canonical operator procedure is
`docs/runbooks/thread_intelligence.md`; the Phase 3/4 carryover record is
`docs/evidence/phase4-closeout/phase3-4-closeout-ledger.md`.

The redacted Task 12 live report is
`docs/evidence/phase4-closeout/thread-intelligence-live-eval.md`, with the human
rubric in `thread-intelligence-live-rubric.json`. The selected contract is
reasoning effort `low` with a `16000` output-token ceiling: 46/46 runs completed,
149/149 material claims were supported, and there were zero incomplete
responses, private-sentinel transmissions, or fabricated decisions.

Browser/mobile/no-JS/accessibility, security/privacy, worker concurrency,
migration/upgrade, backup/restore, and data-preserving runtime rollback remain
pre-flip evidence gates. This ADR therefore records an implemented, evaluated
but still default-off surface; only the later graduation change may update the
activation map and default posture.

## Consequences

- This is the separate processor and automatic-publication decision that the
  Phase 4 plan required; it does not silently erase or reinterpret the earlier
  human-only release boundary.
- The product accepts provider cost and third-party processing only within the
  public-only, budgeted, evidence-bound scope. Sites without credentials retain
  manual memory and deterministic context without provider calls.
- Human approval is a recovery and editorial-control path, not a mandatory gate
  on every generation. Local validation, moderation, provenance, last-good
  behavior, and operator pause/rollback are therefore release blockers.
- Any future expansion to private content, per-member generation, browsing or
  tools, generated posts/actions, a second provider host, or automatic model
  escalation requires a new decision record.
